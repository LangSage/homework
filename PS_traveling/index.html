Explanation

Past Simple

Positive

Use Past Simple for finished actions in the past.

Regular: visit -> visited, stay -> stayed, walk -> walked.

Irregular: go -> went, see -> saw, eat -> ate, buy -> bought, take -> took, have -> had.

Negative

Use didn't + verb.

I didn't visit the museum. He didn't buy a ticket.

Важно: после didn't ставим первую форму глагола. Не didn't went, а didn't go.

Questions

Use Did + person + verb?

Did you travel last summer? Did she buy souvenirs?

Did уже показывает прошлое время, поэтому главный глагол не меняется.

Was / Were

am / is -> was. are -> were.

The hotel was clean. The tickets were expensive.

Was the hotel good? Were the people friendly?

Signal words: yesterday, last week, last month, last year, last summer, two days ago, in 2020, when I was a child.

Reading

Why Do People Travel?

People travel for many reasons. Some people travel because they want to rest. They go to the sea, lie on the beach, swim, and forget about work or study. Other people travel because they want to learn something new. They visit museums, old streets, castles, churches, and famous places.

Some people travel to meet family or friends. Some travel for work. Some travel because they want adventure. They climb mountains, sleep in tents, walk in forests, or visit small villages far from big cities.

Travel is not always perfect. Tickets can be expensive. Hotels can be dirty. Trains can be late. Tourists can lose money, documents, or luggage. Sometimes the worst trip becomes the best story.

Story

The Holiday That Went Wrong

Last summer, Pavel and Igor decided to travel to Saint Petersburg for three days. They bought train tickets online and booked a cheap hostel near the city centre.

The trip started badly. Pavel woke up late and almost missed the train. He ran to the station with his suitcase in one hand and a sandwich in the other. Igor waited near the platform and shouted, "Hurry up!" Pavel jumped into the train two minutes before it left.

When they arrived, Igor couldn't find his wallet. He checked his bag, jacket, and pockets, but the wallet wasn't there. He lost his bank card and some cash.

The hostel photos online looked great, but the real hostel was different. The room was small, the window didn't open, and the bathroom was dirty.

In the evening, it started raining, but they didn't have umbrellas. They got wet and cold. Finally, they found a small cafe near a metro station. The food was simple, but hot and tasty.

The next day was better. They visited a museum, took photos near the river, watched the bridges at night, blocked the bank card, and bought a cheap umbrella. They called the trip a real adventure.

Tourist Mistakes

Tourists often take too many things, forget chargers, lose documents, or don't check the weather. A good tourist checks tickets, passport, money, hotel address, weather, transport, and emergency phone numbers.

Сложная идея: Past Simple помогает рассказывать проблему как цепочку событий: first we arrived, then we lost the address, after that we called the hotel.

Past Simple Help

Positive: I visited. / I went.

Negative: I didn't visit. / I didn't go.

Question: Did you visit? / Did you go?

Be: was / were. The hotel was clean. The people were friendly.

После did / didn't глагол всегда в первой форме: Did you go? I didn't buy.
